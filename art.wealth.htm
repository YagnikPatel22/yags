The 5 Types of Wealth

Money is just one form of wealth. Discover the five types that truly matter and how to build a rich life across all dimensions.

Redefining Wealth

We live in a world that equates wealth with money. But true wealth is measured across multiple dimensions, and financial wealth is just one piece of the puzzle.

The Five Types of Wealth

1. Social Wealth: The strength of your relationships and community. This includes deep friendships, family bonds, and meaningful connections. No amount of money can replace the joy of genuine human connection.

2. Time Wealth: The freedom to spend your time as you choose. It's not about having unlimited time, but having control over how you use it. The wealthiest people guard their time fiercely.

3. Physical Wealth: Your health and vitality. No amount of money can compensate for poor health. Energy, strength, and wellness are the foundation for everything else in life.

4. Mental Wealth: Peace of mind, emotional well-being, and mental clarity. This is the foundation for everything else. Without mental wealth, no other form of wealth truly matters.

5. Financial Wealth: Yes, money matters. But it's a tool to build the other four types of wealth, not an end in itself. Money provides options, security, and freedom—but only when used intentionally.

Building True Wealth

The wealthiest people I know aren't necessarily the richest. They're the ones who have achieved balance across all five dimensions. Start tracking your wealth across all five types, and make intentional choices to build each one.
