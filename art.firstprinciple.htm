First Principles Thinking

Break down complex problems to their fundamental truths. This is how Elon Musk, Aristotle, and other great thinkers approach challenges.

What Is First Principles Thinking?

First principles thinking is the practice of breaking down complex problems into their most basic, foundational elements—the "first principles"—and rebuilding from there.

Instead of reasoning by analogy (doing things because that's how they've always been done), first principles thinking asks: What are the fundamental truths? What do we know to be absolutely true?

The Elon Musk Example

When Elon Musk wanted to build SpaceX, everyone told him rockets were too expensive. The going rate to buy a rocket was $65 million. Instead of accepting this, he asked: What are rockets made of? What is the raw material cost?

He discovered that the materials cost was only about 2% of the typical rocket price. The rest was markup, inefficiency, and legacy costs. By rebuilding from first principles, SpaceX was able to reduce costs by 10x.

"The next time you face a complex problem, resist the urge to reason by analogy. Go back to first principles."

How to Apply It

1. Identify and challenge your assumptions
2. Break the problem down to fundamental truths
3. Create new solutions from the ground up

This approach works for business problems, personal challenges, and strategic decisions. The next time you face a complex problem, resist the urge to reason by analogy. Go back to first principles.
